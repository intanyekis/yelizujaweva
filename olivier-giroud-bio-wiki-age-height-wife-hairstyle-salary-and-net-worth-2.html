    Citing this, Olivier’s club their first ever Ligue 1 title before moving to Arsenal. Here, he won the FA Cup with Arsenal in 2014, 2015, and 2017. Generally, he totaled 105 goals in 253 games for the club.

    Giroud transferred to Chelsea on an 18-month contract and made his full international debut for France in 2011. Thereafter, he became part of the teams which reached the quarter-finals at UEFA Euro 2012 and the 2014 FIFA World Cup.

    During this time, Olivier finished as the joint second-highest goalscorer of the final of UEFA Euro 2016. Arsenal won the race to sign him on a long-term contract for £9.6 million (€12.4 million) in 2012. He was given the number 12 shirt.

    Most noteworthy, Giroud made his debut as a substitute for fellow debutant Lukas Podolski on 18th August. Moreover, he helped Arsenal to a historic comeback in a League Cup tie against Reading, as Arsenal were 4–0 down.

    Later, Olivier scored his first UEFA Champions League goal for Arsenal in a 2–2 draw at Schalke. Citing this, he earned him a place in the Premier League team of the week. He scored as Arsenal fought back to earn a 2–2 draw, from 2–0 down at home to Liverpool.

    Furthermore, Giroud was awarded the Arsenal Fan’s Player of the Month Award. He played a big part in Arsenal’s 3–1 win against Norwich City at home in 2013. He received his first red card for Arsenal against Fulham and missed three of Arsenal’s last four games of the season.

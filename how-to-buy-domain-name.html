    The first action towards creating a website is to get a good domain name and register it. In this article, we will explain to you how to register a domain name step by step.

    What is a Domain Name?

    A domain name is your website’s address that people type in the browser to visit your website—for instance, Artimization.com.

    In simple terms, if your website was a house, then your domain name will be its address.

    The entire internet is a massive network of computers. Each computer is specified a number which is called an IP address, and it looks like this: 104.26.12.238

    Now, this is not simple to remember the address. Imagine if you had to use this kind of address to visit your desired websites.

    To fix this problem, domain names were created. Domain names can have alphabets and numbers, which supports business owners to create brandable names for their website’s address.

    Few Best Suggestions Before Buying Domain Names:

    Before you buy a domain name, consider doing some proper research. After all, the name of your website is one of the prime things that visitors will see. Here are some essential points to keep in mind:

  • Do your research: It’s essential to analyze your niche and to know what the user requires. Being aware of the most popular keywords and search terms will support you to find a domain name that is the best fit to represent your project.
  • Make it attractive and easy to remember: Do not go for something that is long or hard to pronounce. A catchy and concise domain name will significantly increase the chances of visitors memorizing your website name.
  • Go with .com if possible: Currently, .com is taking a stand as the most famous domain name extension. Thus, it is a good preference when buying a domain name, as most web users will easily recognize it.
  • Check for trademark infringement: To avoid any legal issues, it is best not to register a domain like popular, well-established brands. You can use various online tools to look up trademark information and bypass such situations.
  • Avoid numbers and hyphens: While numbers and hyphens allow you to create more domain name changes, they can sometimes do more harm than good. For instance, people can confuse the number zero (0) with the letter (o) or the number (1) for a word (one). Therefore eliminating numbers and hyphens may be a good approach.
  • Look for close options: If you already have a domain name in mind, think of possible mistyped variations to ensure all the potential traffic reaches you. A great example would be amzon.com which takes you to the main Amazon website, or fb.com, which moves directly to Facebook.
  • Research social media platforms: Once you have a rough outline of your domain name, look it up on famous social media platforms like Twitter, YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook. Even if you are not intending to do anything with them at the start, keeping a page for your project will ensure it is not taken by someone else.
  • Think future-proof: Websites and domains are a long-term investment. Hence spinning the name around the most overhyped thing at this very time might not be the best strategy in the long run. So do not be scared to think outside the box.

    • Review your ideas: If you feel like picking the right name for your website is the most challenging choice of your life, possibly you need some help. Give your ideas to friends, family, colleagues. Who understands, they might give you that golden piece of advice that you are looking for.

    How much does it cost to buy a domain name?

    Usually, a .com domain name costs $14.99 / year. The cost of domain changes is based on each different extension. Some are even more expensive than this.

    We do not suggest using anything but .com domains because everybody remembers a .com domain, and your smartphone keyboards have a pre-built key for .com

    Things to Watch Out for When Choosing a Domain Name Registrar

    Due to the high demand in the domain registration business, you will come across companies with some attractive offers. While these offers may look good, there may be some downsides that you may overlook right away.

    Following are a few things you must watch out for before choosing a domain name registrar.

    1. Add-on Pricing

    Some domain name registrars can automatically pre-check add-on services when you buy a domain name. If you do not uncheck those options, then this would raise your bill significantly. Typically a .com domain name should not cost you more than $14.99 / year. If it does, then it should show a red flag.

    You must pay consideration to the checkout page and uncheck any add-on services that you do not require. On top of that, it is always a good approach to double-check the total amount you are charged in your credit card statements.

    2. Bad User Experience

    Most domain registrars attempt to make their website easy to use, so they can keep you as a customer. However, some domain registrars do the exact opposite.

    This would make it hard for you to change your domain name settings. You will require to do that if you wanted to transfer the domain to a new host or a new domain registrar. The simplest way to look for this is by searching for domain registrar’s online reviews. Keep in mind that very few happy customers leave online reviews, so it is essential to read the reviews thoroughly.

    3. Hidden Fees

    You may also require to look for the hidden charges and fees when picking a domain registrar.

    Some domain registrars may have unique offers for more affordable domains for first-time registrations. Most beginners do not realize it, but the domain’s renewal cost is often quite unconventional and even higher.

    All good domain registrars make it simple to transfer your domain name to a different company. However, some will put an additional fee.

    That being said, let’s take a look at the best domain name registrar that will benefit you in future.

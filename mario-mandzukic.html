    Mario Mandžukic is a professional footballer from Croatia who started playing for his national side Croatia’s senior team in November 2007. Before that, he played for Croatia’s U21, U20, and U19 teams. He has also played for the clubs Marsonia, VfL Wolfsburg, Bayern Munich, Atlético Madrid, Juventus, and many others. In 2012 and 2013, he was honored with “Croatian Footballer of the Year” title.

    Best Known For

    • His trophy-laden stint with Dinamo Zagreb. He managed to win 3 Croatian league titles as well as 3 Croatian Football Cups with the Zagreb based club.
    • His stay with the Bavarian giants, Bayern Munich. He won 2 Bundesliga titles, 2 DFB-Pokal titles, FIFA Club World Cup, as well as UEFA Champions League with the club.
    • Having played for Italian giant, Juventus FC. He managed to win 3 consecutive Serie A titles as well as 3 consecutive Coppa Italia cups with the Turin based club.

    First Soccer Match

    In 2004, he made his professional debut for the Croatian club, Marsonia.

    In August 2012, he made his official debut for Bayern Munich in the German DFL Supercup match against Borussia Dortmund. He scored a goal in the 6th minute to help his side win the match.

    In August 2015, he made his first official start for Juventus in their 1-0 defeat to Udinese.

    In November 2007, he made his international debut for Croatia in a friendly match against Macedonia.

    Mario Mandžukic Facts

  • When he was very young, his family moved to Germany as the Yugoslav Wars was causing mayhem in their native Yugoslavia.
  • Later, his family moved back to Croatia after it had just gained independence. He started playing soccer for NK Marsonia.
  • His strong performances for NK Zagreb under the guidance of Miroslav Blažević drew the attention from some of the largest European clubs including Chelsea and Manchester United.
  • In 2007, the Croatian giants Dinamo Zagreb purchased him for €1.3 million as they sought to replace striker Eduardo, who had moved to Arsenal FC.
  • In June 2012, he was purchased from Wolfsburg by Bayern Munich for €13 million. They were impressed by his performance for German club and at the UEFA Euro 2012 competition.
  • In 2013 UEFA Champions League final, he scored his side’s first goal in their 2-1 victory over Borussia Dortmund. The goal made him the first Croatian player to score in the final.
  • In June 2015, he moved to Juventus from Bayern Munich in a deal worth €19 million, which was payable in three installments. They also agreed further €2 million in add-ons.
  • In June 2017, he scored a stunning goal for Juventus to brought his side level with Real Madrid. He had flicked the ball over the shoulder from the distance of around 15 yards to score one of the finest goals in the history of the competition.
  • In May 2017, he agreed on a contract extension with Juventus management to extend his potential stay at the club to 2020.
  • In the summer of 2018, he was linked with Manchester United as it was claimed that Jose Mourinho was looking for Romelu Lukaku‘s backup.

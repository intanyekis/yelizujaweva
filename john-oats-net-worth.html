    Early Life

    John Oates, a renowned American rock guitarist, musician, and songwriter, was born on April 7, 1949, in New York City. However, he spent his formative years in North Wales, Pennsylvania, a suburb of Philadelphia. Oates came from a diverse background with his mother being an Italian immigrant and his father having English and Gibraltarian roots.

    During his school days, Oates developed a passion for wrestling and even went on to captain his high school’s wrestling team. His dedication and prowess in the sport garnered him wrestling scholarships from various colleges. Ultimately, he decided to pursue a different path and enrolled at Temple University in Philadelphia to focus on his musical aspirations.

    Throughout his freshman year, Oates made the significant decision to transition away from wrestling and devote himself entirely to his music career, setting in motion a remarkable journey that would make him a household name in the music industry.

    “I had to make a choice between music and sports, and I chose music. And I always laugh because I could have been a professional wrestler, maybe.”

    Hall & Oates

    While studying at Temple University, John Oates met Daryl Hall, a senior at the university. In 1967, they formed the music duo Hall & Oates. They released their first albums in the early 1970s but found greater success when they signed with Atlantic Records in 1972. Hall & Oates went on to release several albums and achieved commercial success with hits like “Sara Smile,” “She’s Gone,” “Rich Girl,” and “Maneater.” They became one of the best-selling music duos of all time, selling 40 million records worldwide.

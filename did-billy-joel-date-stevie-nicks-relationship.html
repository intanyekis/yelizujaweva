Despite never publicly confirming or denying their supposed romance, fans have continued to speculate about their connection. The truth does go a bit beyond what these stars have admitted to.

    A Timeline Of Billy Joel And Stevie Nick's Dating History

    When it comes to whether Billy Joel and Stevie Nicks ever dated each other, relationship history and evidence play a crucial role in answering this question. While there have been rumors circulating for decades about the two iconic musicians being romantically involved with each other, there is little concrete evidence to support these claims.

    However, delving deeper into their respective relationship histories can provide us with some insights into their personal lives and potential connections.

    Joel has had an interesting romantic history throughout his life. According to People, he has been married four times and has had several high-profile relationships with celebrities such as Christie Brinkley and Elle Macpherson.

    His first marriage was to Elizabeth Weber Small in 1973, whom he met while working as a piano bar pianist in Los Angeles. They separated after nine years of marriage. He then went on to marry model Christie Brinkley in 1985 after they met on the set of his music video "Uptown Girl". The couple had a daughter together but eventually divorced in 1994.

    Nicks, who nowadays might be more known for her friendship with Harry Style and her fandom of Taylor Swift, has also had her fair share of romantic entanglements throughout her life.

    According to Rolling Stone, Nicks dated Eagles' frontman Don Henley during the late '70s and early '80s while still being involved with fellow Fleetwood Mac band member Mick Fleetwood. Her tumultuous relationship with Fleetwood lasted for several years before it ultimately ended for a multitude of chaotic reasons.

    Some of Nick's most iconic songs are based on those relationships and the struggles involved.

    Did Billy Joel And Stevie Nicks Ever Date?

    In an interview with Rolling Stone magazine in 2019, Nicks spoke highly of Joel and their friendship over the years. She mentioned that they had stayed in touch and that she admired him greatly as a songwriter and musician. However, she did not divulge any information about their personal relationship or whether they had ever dated each other.

    The speculation about their alleged relationship has been fueled by various sources, including tabloids, interviews, and even some of their respective songs. However, despite the persistent rumors, there is no concrete evidence to suggest that the two musicians ever dated each other.

    The early friendship and musical collaborations between Nicks and Joel were not only the foundation of their long-lasting relationship but also a significant milestone in the music industry. Their bond began in the late 1970s when they met at a recording studio in New York City. At that time, Joel was already an established artist with several hit songs under his belt, while Nicks was on the rise as a member of Fleetwood Mac.

    One of the main sources of speculation about Joel and Nicks' relationship is their collaboration on the song "I Don't Want to Know." The track was included on Fleetwood Mac's 1977 album "Rumours," which was produced by Joel's longtime collaborator Phil Ramone. Some fans have interpreted the song as a thinly veiled confession of love between Joel and Nicks. However, nothing has ever come of these rumors.

Both Joel and Nicks have had incredibly successful careers and enduring legacies in the music industry, so it's not surprising that fans would be curious about their personal lives.

One such example was "I Will Talk and Hollywood Will Listen," which Joel wrote for Nicks's solo album "Trouble in Shangri-La."

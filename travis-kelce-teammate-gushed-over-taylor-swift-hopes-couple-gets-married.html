    Travis Kelce's Teammate Hopes He Marries Taylor Swift

    Contrary to what some vicious haters want to convince everyone of, the Kansas City Chiefs couldn't be happier to have Taylor Swift around. Far from ruining the experience, she's opened the door for new fans to enjoy football, and anyone who loves the sport should be glad about it. Travis' teammates certainly are.

    Recently, Kansas City Chiefs kicker Harrison Butker spoke about the couple, and surprised everyone saying "I hope they get married and start a family." He couldn't stop gushing about the pop star and how glad he is of her presence.

    "She thinks that's amazing that I can kick the ball so far through the uprights!" He gushed. "It was a great experience, and I can't say enough great things about her."

    He also said that, while he's not exactly a swiftie, he was still apprehensive when he heard the singer would be coming to see them. "I was a little nervous to meet Taylor Swift. I mean, it's Taylor Swift. So maybe I am a Swiftie if I was nervous to meet her."

    He's probably talking about the secret meetings before Taylor and Travis' relationship became public, when he took her to the stadium to meet his teammates before she actually showed up at a game. It's good that they did that, because that way the team got used to having a superstar there instead of looking up and finding her watching them. That would be nerve-wracking.

    Travis' Coaches Said Taylor Was A Good Influence On The Team

    It seems in addition to bonding with Travis Kelce's parents, Taylor Swift has also been bonding with the team's crew. No one can deny that the Chiefs coaches' first priority is the team, so them saying she's good for the team means a lot.

    Coach Andy Reid strongly denied that Taylor's presence in the stadium causes any form of distraction or disadvantage. On the contrary, he praised the way she and Travis conduct themselves. "It's a tribute to how she’s handled it, how Kelce has handled it," he stated. "It hasn't been a distraction, so there haven’t been any problems with it."
